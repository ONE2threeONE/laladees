01   Executive Summary

The Opportunity

LaLa Dee's Italian Steakhouse is a premium, chef-driven dining concept opening in Old Town Scottsdale, Arizona in 2026. Founded by cousins Laura and Karen, the restaurant brings together two culinary lineages rooted in Sicily and northern Italy, expressed through handmade pasta, prime and Wagyu beef, and daily-fresh seafood. The result is a dining experience that is equal parts Italian tradition and American steakhouse ambition.

Old Town Scottsdale is one of the most concentrated fine-dining corridors in the American Southwest, drawing over 8 million visitors annually with an average household income among local residents exceeding $115,000. The market is large, the guest is affluent, and the appetite for elevated, story-driven dining concepts is well established. What it lacks is a true Italian steakhouse anchored in genuine culinary heritage and executed at the highest level.

LaLa Dee's fills that gap. We are seeking $950,000 in equity investment to complete our buildout, fund pre-opening operations, and open our doors. We project reaching profitability by month seven, with an annual EBITDA of approximately $374,000 by year two.

02   The Concept

What We Are Building

LaLa Dee's is a 60-seat intimate dining room open Tuesday through Sunday for dinner. The menu lives at the intersection of the Italian trattoria and the American steakhouse: handmade pasta cut fresh each morning, Wagyu and prime beef dry-aged in house, and a raw bar of daily seafood sourced from coastal suppliers. The wine program is Italian-forward, with a curated selection of Barolo, Brunello, and Sicilian reds alongside an Italian-inspired cocktail list.

The room is warm and dramatic. Dark walls, candlelight, the kind of space where a conversation feels private even when the restaurant is full. We are not a white-tablecloth concept, but every detail from the hand-stitched linens to the custom ceramic tableware speaks to genuine care and intention.

La Bistecca

Prime and Wagyu beef, dry-aged in house. Ribeye, filet, bistecca Fiorentina. Finished with Italian-style compound butters and seasonal accompaniments.

La Pasta

All pasta rolled by hand each morning. Pappardelle, tagliatelle, gnocchi, ravioli. Sauces built from whole San Marzano tomatoes, braised meats, and seasonal produce.

Il Mare

Daily fresh seafood flown in from coastal markets. Crudo, branzino, whole roasted fish, linguine alle vongole. The freshest catch served the simplest way.

03   Market Opportunity

Scottsdale Is Ready

Old Town Scottsdale is consistently ranked among the top food and hospitality destinations in the country. The area hosts over 8 million annual visitors and a permanent population with one of the highest concentrations of per-capita restaurant spending in Arizona. Tourism peaks in October through May, coinciding with the winter visitor season and the PGA Tour's Phoenix Open, which alone draws 700,000 guests to the region.

The fine dining segment in Old Town has grown significantly over the past decade, with guests increasingly seeking chef-driven, story-backed concepts over chain steakhouses or generic Italian. Independent restaurants with genuine culinary identity consistently outperform on Google ratings, reservation lead times, and average check.

Despite the market's strength, there is no credible Italian steakhouse in Old Town anchored in authentic Italian culinary heritage. Existing Italian concepts trend casual. Existing steakhouses trend corporate. LaLa Dee's occupies the intersection of both categories and does so with a story that no national brand can replicate.

04   The Team

The People Behind the Table

LaLa Dee's was built by two people who grew up around the same table. Their grandmother was Sicilian, her kitchen a masterclass in boldness, generosity, and patience. Their grandfather was from Nicosa, in the north of Italy, where the food was quieter and the craft ran deeper. Karen and Laura are cousins, best friends, and the living expression of everything those two people built. They did not decide to open a restaurant. The restaurant was always where they were headed.

Laura Co-Founder & Executive Chef

Laura carries her grandmother's fire. She has spent her career in professional kitchens mastering steakhouse technique and the art of handmade pasta, but she cooks from the same place she always has. The heart. She leads the kitchen at LaLa Dee's and sets the standard for every plate that leaves it.

Karen Co-Founder & Culinary Director

Karen has her grandfather's patience and precision. She is the first one in and the last one to leave. She starts the sauces before dawn. She oversees sourcing, recipe development, and the systems that allow every service to run at a level that is both consistent and exceptional.
