Where Sicily Meets
the North of Italy

✦

The story of LaLa Dee's begins with two people from opposite ends of Italy. Their grandmother was born in Sicily, in a kitchen that never went cold. She cooked the way Sicilian women have cooked for centuries: bold, generous, unhurried. Her caponata had fifteen ingredients and four arguments attached to the recipe. Her Sunday gravy started before sunrise and asked you to wait. She believed the table was the most important room in any house, and she spent her whole life proving it.

Their grandfather was from Nicosa, a small village in the north of Italy, where the food was quieter, the winters longer, and the patience ran deeper. He knew how to coax flavor from a bone, how to build a risotto from nothing, how to make a simple thing extraordinary with time and attention and not a single shortcut. He was a man who understood that cooking is a kind of conversation, and he believed in taking his time with it.

These two people found each other. And somewhere at the intersection of Sicily and the north, of bold and patient, of the sea and the mountains, a family was built. Around that family table, two little girls grew up side by side: Karen and Laura, cousins, best friends, and the reason LaLa Dee's exists. They learned everything at that table. They fought over the last cannolo. They stirred the same pots, ruined the same recipes, and were taught by a grandmother who cooked like the world depended on it, and a grandfather who showed them that it does.

The Heart & Soul of the Kitchen

Meet the Chefs

✦
Laura Co-Founder & Executive Chef
✦

Laura got her grandmother's fire. From the time she was small, it was Sicily she felt pulling at her through the kitchen. The deep, sun-darkened tomatoes. The briny olives. The way a simple piece of fish could be transformed into something you would think about for years. Her grandmother taught her that good cooking begins with generosity, and Laura has never forgotten it.

She carried those roots into every kitchen she ever worked in, mastering the craft of the steakhouse and the art of handmade pasta, but always cooking from the same place her grandmother cooked from. That place is the heart. She built LaLa Dee's so that every guest who walks through the door feels what she felt at that Sicilian table. Welcomed. Fed. Loved.

She always says the same thing before service begins. Cuciniamo con amore. We cook with love.

Karen Co-Founder & Culinary Director
✦

Karen is her grandfather's granddaughter. She has his patience, his precision, his belief that a great dish is never rushed. Growing up, she was the one who stayed in the kitchen after everyone else had gone, sitting beside him, watching his hands, asking questions he was always happy to answer. Nicosa taught her that cooking is not performance. It is practice. It is the same sauce made a thousand times until it becomes something you could not imagine living without.

But she is Sicilian too, in the blood and in the bone, and she carries her grandmother with her every single day. The boldness. The generosity. The absolute insistence that nobody leaves the table hungry. Karen puts both of them into every plate she makes. The north and the south of Italy, held together by love and by fire.

At LaLa Dee's, she is the first one in and the last one to leave. She starts the ragù at dawn. She rolls the pasta by hand, the way her grandfather showed her before she was tall enough to reach the counter.
